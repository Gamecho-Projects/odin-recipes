Molly's favourite chicken noodle soup

Photo by Jana Ohajdova

There’s something deeply comforting about a steaming bowl of chicken noodle soup, especially when it’s made with a rich, brothy base that warms you from the inside out. This version takes that classic coziness and gives it a little edge, with a clear golden broth, tender noodles, and just enough heat from fresh chillies to brighten every spoonful. It’s the kind of dish that feels restorative and satisfying without being heavy. You’ll want to curl up with on achilly evening or enjoy as a soothing reset after a long day.

What really elevates this soup, though, is the finishing touch of fresh herbs scattered over the top just before serving. The garnish adds freshness and color, balancing the gentle spice and bringing the broth to life. Every bite combines tender chicken, slippery noodles, and fragrant herbs, all tied together by the warmth of the broth. It’s simple, wholesome cooking at its best—one pot, minimal fuss, and maximum flavor.

Ingredient

  • 3 ½ cups chicken broth
  • ½ red bell pepper, cut into 2-inch-long strips
  • 2 green onions, sliced diagonally
  • 1 medium carrot, sliced diagonally
  • 1 stalk celery, sliced diagonally
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon soy sauce
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • 1 pinch black pepper
  • 1 cup cooked, shredded boneless, skinless chicken breast meat
  • 4 ounces uncooked ramen noodles, broken into pieces

Directions for cooking

  1. Combine broth, bell pepper, green onions, carrot, celery, garlic, soy sauce, ginger, and pepper in a 2-quart saucepan over medium-high heat; bring to a boil.
  2. Simmer until vegetables soft and flavours incorporated
  3. Stir chicken and noodles into the pan, reduce the heat to medium, and cook until noodles are tender, about 10 minutes.
